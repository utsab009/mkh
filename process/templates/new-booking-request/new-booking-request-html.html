What Happens Next / How do I meet with my Mentee?

Your Mentee will be sent your Skype address as part of the message telling them that you have accepted this meeting request. If you have not given Try A Mentor your Skype address, before you accept this request, go to TryAMentor.com, sign in, and click on the circle containing your photo or initials on the top right hand corner of the page. A drop-down menu will appear, click 'Mentor Profile', scroll down to 'Skype Link' and place your Skype Address here and then accept the meeting request. If you are not sure how to use Skype or find your Skype address, simply google these questions and a host of helpful videos will aid you.

What Equipment will I need?

Your computer, tablet or mobile phone / cell will need access to effective internet speed along with having a camera, speakers, and microphone (they tend to be built into modern mobile phones / cells, tablets, and computers).

A Great Way to Avoid or Solve Problems

Before your meeting is scheduled to start, open Try A Mentor, sign-in and go to your Inbox (From Mentees). You can type a message to your Mentor to say you are about to connect, and they can reply in real time. This way you can offer help to each other if you run into any problems.

Can Try A Mentor help me to Mentor?

Yes, indirectly. Sign into Try A Mentor and click on the circle containing your photo or initials on the top right-hand corner of the page. A drop-down menu will appear, click 'Mentor Resources'. Here you will find a range of resources to help you mentor including an on-line training programme (free of charge) on how to Mentor.

What if I do not want to use Skype?

No problem. You must still provide a Skype address in the Mentor Profile (just in case). But then, after you accept meeting requests, sign into Try A Mentor and go to your inbox (from Mentees). Click on any messages from your Mentee (e.g. this meeting request). Here you can send them additional messages. You can tell them here that you want to use a different video conferencing application (you can never meet face to face) e.g. Zoom and provide them with the information to use this application and provide them with the means to meet you on it

When will I get paid?

Payment is made precisely fourteen days after each Mentoring meeting you perform unless disputed by the Mentee. It will then usually take up to seven days to reach your bank.
